# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer shopping behavior using transactional retail data to uncover actionable business insights. The analysis was performed using **Python, PostgreSQL, and Power BI** to identify trends in customer purchasing patterns, subscription behavior, product performance, discount usage, and customer loyalty.

The project simulates a real-world retail business problem where management aims to improve:

- Customer engagement
- Revenue generation
- Product positioning
- Marketing effectiveness
- Customer retention strategies

The complete workflow includes:

- Data Cleaning & Transformation using Python
- Business Query Analysis using SQL
- Interactive Dashboard Development using Power BI
- Business Insight Generation & Recommendations

---

# Business Problem Statement

A retail company wants to better understand its customers’ shopping behavior across demographics, product categories, discounts, reviews, and shipping preferences.

The objective is to answer:

> “How can consumer shopping data be leveraged to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?”

## Business Questions Solved

### 1. Revenue Contribution by Gender

Analyzed total revenue generated by male and female customers.

### Insight
Female customers generated slightly higher revenue compared to male customers.

### 2. High-Spending Discount Users

Identified customers who used discounts but still spent above the average purchase amount.

### Insight
Discounts successfully attracted high-value customers instead of only price-sensitive buyers.

### 3. Top Rated Products

Identified products with the highest average review ratings.

### Top Performing Products

- Sandals
- Boots
- Hat

---

### 4. Shipping Preference Analysis

Compared purchase behavior between Standard and Express shipping users.

### Insight
Customers choosing Express Shipping had a higher average purchase amount.

### 5. Subscription Analysis

Compared spending patterns between subscribers and non-subscribers.

### Insight
Subscribers contributed significantly to long-term customer value and retention.

### 6. Discount Dependency Analysis

Identified products most frequently purchased using discounts.

### Insight

# Sample SQL Query

```sql
SELECT subscription_status,
       COUNT(customer_id) AS total_customers,
       ROUND(AVG(purchase_amount),2) AS avg_spend,
       ROUND(SUM(purchase_amount),2) AS total_revenue
FROM customer
GROUP BY subscription_status;
```
